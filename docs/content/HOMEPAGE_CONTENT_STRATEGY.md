# Homepage Content Strategy

## georgeungureanu.doctor

---

## The Homepage's Job

The homepage is not a summary of the website. It is a conversation opener.

A patient lands here for one of three reasons:
1. They have a condition or symptom they are worried about and are evaluating doctors
2. They were referred by another doctor and are forming a first impression
3. They were told about this doctor by someone they trust and are confirming the choice

In all three cases, the homepage must do the same thing in the same order:

> **Reassure → Orient → Introduce → Direct**

---

## The Narrative Arc

The homepage tells a patient story, not a doctor story.

```
1. You have a problem and you are worried.          [Acknowledge]
2. You have come to the right place.                [Reassure]
3. Here is what we treat.                           [Orient]
4. Here is who will take care of you.               [Introduce]
5. Here is why you can trust this doctor.           [Build trust]
6. Here is what other patients experienced.         [Social proof]
7. Here is how to prepare for your visit.           [Reduce friction]
8. Here is where to find more information.          [Resources]
9. Here is how to take the next step.              [Direct to action]
```

## Section-by-Section Content Strategy

### Hero Section

**Purpose:** Create immediate emotional relevance and calm. The patient should feel, in 5 seconds: "This is for me."

**What the headline must do:**
- Address the patient's concern, not the doctor's achievement
- Be specific enough to confirm relevance
- Be calm enough to reduce anxiety rather than add to it

**Headline direction:**
- NOT: "Excellence in Neurosurgery"
- NOT: "Prof. Dr. George Ungureanu — Neurosurgeon"
- DIRECTION: Something that begins with the patient's reality

Example orientations (not final copy):
- "When neurological symptoms affect your daily life, understanding your options is the first step."
- "Expert neurosurgical care, explained clearly. So you can make the right decision."
- "Clarity. Precision. Care. When you need neurosurgery, you deserve all three."

**Sub-headline:** 1–2 sentences confirming specialty and approach without credentials-listing.

**CTAs:** Two — primary (Book a consultation) and secondary (Learn about conditions). Primary is more prominent but neither is aggressive.

---

### Patient Promise / Intro Section

**Purpose:** Establish the philosophy of care in one short, human paragraph.

**This section is not:**
- A mission statement
- A list of services
- A credential section

**It is:**
- 2–3 sentences that speak directly to what the patient can expect
- The doctor's voice, not institutional language

Example orientation (not final copy):
> "Every patient who comes to me is navigating something difficult. My goal is to give you the same clear, honest information I would give a member of my own family — so that whatever decision you face, you face it fully informed."

---

### Conditions Section

**Purpose:** Help patients quickly identify whether their condition is one this doctor treats.

**Content strategy:**
- 6–8 most common or most important conditions
- Each card: plain-language condition name, 1 sentence of what it is in patient language, link
- Section headline is a patient-facing question, not a category label:
  - NOT: "Conditions Treated"
  - YES: "Do you recognize any of these conditions?"
  - YES: "What conditions do we specialize in?"

**Conditions likely to be featured (to be confirmed with Dr. Ungureanu):**
- Brain tumors (benign and malignant)
- Spinal disc herniation
- Cerebrovascular conditions (aneurysms, AVM)
- Hydrocephalus
- Carpal tunnel syndrome and peripheral nerve conditions
- Trigeminal neuralgia
- Spinal stenosis
- Meningioma

---

### About the Doctor Section

**Purpose:** Build personal trust. Make the doctor feel like a real person, not a title.

**Content strategy:**
- Photo must be warm and present — not the doctor in a white coat staring at a camera
- Biography teaser: 3–4 sentences that answer "who is this person and why does he do this work?"
- The word "I" should appear — the doctor speaks directly

**What to include:**
- Why he chose neurosurgery (his motivation — human, not career-focused)
- What his philosophy of patient care is
- What makes his approach different

**What to exclude from this teaser:**
- Credential lists (on the About page)
- Conference history
- Publication count

---

### Credibility / Stats Section

**Purpose:** Establish clinical experience through numbers — but framed for patients, not peers.

**Numbers to use (to be confirmed):**
- Years of practice
- Procedures performed
- Publications (brief — frames expertise without being academic)
- Institutional affiliations (1–2 maximum)

**Framing rule:** Numbers serve patient reassurance, not professional prestige.
- NOT: "12 years of academic research"
- YES: "12 years of specialized neurosurgical practice"

---

### Patient Testimonials Section (If Available)

**Purpose:** Social proof through real patient voice.

**Content rules:**
- Specific, not generic: "After my surgery, I was back to hiking in 6 weeks" beats "Dr. Ungureanu is wonderful"
- Attributed: first name and condition minimum (with explicit permission)
- Honest: not every testimonial needs to be about a miraculous recovery — "I finally felt like someone explained my condition to me" is valuable
- Maximum 3 testimonials on homepage
- No star ratings

**If testimonials are not yet available:**
- Omit this section entirely — do not use fake or generic testimonials
- Add placeholder in the template for when real testimonials are collected

---

### Patient Information / Appointment Prep Section

**Purpose:** Reduce friction before the appointment. Show patients the process is manageable.

**Content strategy:**
- 3–4 steps or items
- Icons for visual scannability
- Focus on reducing the unknown: "Here is what happens next"

Example items:
- Contact us by phone or form
- We confirm your appointment within 24 hours
- Bring your previous scans and medical records
- Your first consultation lasts approximately 45 minutes

---

### Patient Resources / Articles Section

**Purpose:** Establish thought leadership and provide genuine value for patients who want more information.

**Content strategy:**
- 3 most useful or most recent articles
- Titles are patient-facing questions or plain descriptions:
  - NOT: "Meningioma: Epidemiology and Surgical Outcomes"
  - YES: "What to Expect After Brain Tumor Surgery"
  - YES: "Spinal Disc Herniation: When Is Surgery Actually Necessary?"
- Include reading time — respects the patient's time and sets expectations

---

### Final CTA Section

**Purpose:** Close the homepage with a clear, calm invitation to act.

**Content strategy:**
- Headline: Direct and encouraging, not urgent or pressuring
- Sub-text: Removes the last anxiety — what does "booking a consultation" actually mean?
- CTA button: "Book a consultation" — primary and prominent

**Example orientation (not final copy):**
> **Ready to understand your options?**
> A consultation with Dr. Ungureanu is the first step. During your first appointment, we will review your situation together, answer your questions, and discuss the right path forward for you.
> [Book a Consultation]

## Content to Avoid on the Homepage

| Avoid | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| Award logos and badges | Ego-forward; not patient-facing |
| Media mentions or press logos | Same reason |
| Long credential lists | Belongs on About page |
| Aggressive appointment CTAs | Creates pressure, not trust |
| Testimonial carousels with auto-scroll | Accessibility issue; also feels like marketing |
| Stock photos of operating rooms | Cold, impersonal, clichéd |
| "State-of-the-art" language | Empty, expected, trusted by no one |
| Animations on primary content | Distracts from the message |

---

## Mobile Homepage Priority

On mobile, the content hierarchy compresses. The following must be fully visible above the fold on a 375px device:

1. Logo / navigation
2. Hero headline
3. Primary CTA button

Everything else scrolls. Mobile users tap quickly — make every section earn its scroll position.
