# Content Structure

## georgeungureanu.doctor

---

## Overview

This document defines the content structure for each page type on the website. It specifies what sections exist, what each section contains, and in what order. This is not a copywriting document — it is a structural framework that guides content creation and page building.

---

## Global Content Elements

These elements appear on every page.

### Header (Global)

- Logo (Doctor's name / site mark)
- Primary navigation
- Appointment CTA button

### Footer (Global)

- Doctor name and specialty
- Short practice description (1–2 sentences)
- Navigation links (main pages)
- Contact information (phone, address, email)
- Appointment CTA
- Copyright and legal links

## Page: Homepage

The homepage serves patients who are new to the doctor — searching, evaluating, deciding. It must do four things: reassure, orient, introduce, and direct.

### Section 1: Hero

- Headline: Patient-facing, empathetic, not self-promotional
- Sub-headline: Briefly establishes specialty and approach
- Primary CTA: Book a consultation
- Secondary CTA: Learn about conditions
- Background: Professional doctor photography or calm abstract

**Tone test:** Does the headline speak to the patient's concern, not the doctor's achievement?

### Section 2: Patient Promise / Intro

- Brief statement of what this practice offers the patient
- 2–3 sentences maximum
- No credentials here — only what this means for the patient

### Section 3: Conditions Overview

- Section overline: e.g., "What We Treat"
- Section headline: Patient-facing (e.g., "Conditions We Specialize In")
- Grid of 6–8 condition cards
- Each card: condition name + 1-sentence plain-language description + link to condition page
- Section CTA: View all conditions

### Section 4: About the Doctor (Teaser)

- Doctor photo (warm, approachable — not clinical)
- Brief human biography: who he is, what drives him, his approach to patients
- 3–4 sentences maximum — more detail on the About page
- Link to full About page

### Section 5: Stats / Credibility

- 3–4 impact numbers: years of experience, number of procedures, academic publications, etc.
- Numbers must be verifiable and current
- Frame numbers from the patient perspective: "X years helping patients" not "X years of practice"

### Section 6: What Patients Say (Optional — if testimonials are available)

- 2–3 patient testimonials
- First-person, specific, honest — not marketing-speak
- Attribution: first name, condition (with patient permission)
- No star ratings

### Section 7: Patient Information / Appointment Prep

- Section headline: "Preparing for Your Visit" or "What to Expect"
- 3–4 key points with icons: what to bring, what happens at consultation, how to prepare
- CTA: Read full patient information

### Section 8: Latest Articles / Resources

- Section headline: "Understanding Your Condition" or "Patient Resources"
- 3 most recent or most relevant articles
- Brief excerpt, date, reading time
- CTA: View all resources

### Section 9: Final CTA

- Strong, calm headline: "Ready to take the next step?"
- Sub-text: reassuring, not pressuring
- Primary CTA: Book a consultation

## Page: About Dr. Ungureanu

### Section 1: Hero

- Page title
- 1-line specialty descriptor

### Section 2: Doctor Introduction

- Large photo
- Full name, specialty, and institutional affiliation
- 3–4 paragraph biography
- Written in third person but warm — not a CV recitation
- Must include: why he chose neurosurgery, what drives his approach to patients, what patients can expect from him

### Section 3: Approach to Care

- 3–4 values or principles with brief explanation
- Example: "I believe every patient deserves to understand their condition in plain language."
- Human voice, not institutional language

### Section 4: Education and Training

- Structured list: degree, institution, year
- Clean, readable — not overwhelming

### Section 5: Specializations

- Bulleted or tagged list of subspecialty areas
- Brief description of each (1 sentence)

### Section 6: Academic Publications

- List of selected publications
- Proper academic citation format
- Brief lay-language summary of each (1 sentence)

### Section 7: Memberships and Affiliations

- Professional society memberships
- Hospital affiliations
- Academic appointments

### Section 8: CTA

- Book a consultation

## Page: Individual Condition

Each condition has its own dedicated page.

### Section 1: Hero

- Condition name (clear, plain)
- 1-sentence summary of what it is

### Section 2: What Is This Condition?

- Plain-language explanation
- Who it affects
- What causes it (if known)
- What it feels like (symptoms in patient language)

### Section 3: Diagnosis

- How is it diagnosed?
- What tests or scans are typically done?
- What should a patient expect at diagnosis?

### Section 4: Treatment Options

- What are the treatment options?
- When is surgery recommended?
- What happens if it's not treated?

### Section 5: The Procedure (If Surgical)

- What does the surgery involve?
- How long does it take?
- Is it done under general anesthesia?
- What is the technique?

### Section 6: Recovery

- What does recovery look like?
- What is the typical hospital stay?
- When can the patient go home?
- When can they return to work / normal activity?
- What follow-up is required?

### Section 7: FAQ

- 4–6 most common patient questions about this condition
- Answered in plain language

### Section 8: CTA

- Book a consultation about this condition

## Content Rules (All Pages)

1. Every page has exactly one primary CTA
2. Every condition page links to the appointment page
3. No page ends without a next step for the patient
4. All medical terms defined on first use per page
5. No page requires more than 3 navigation clicks to reach from the homepage
6. Headings answer questions, not describe categories
