# Project Brief

## georgeungureanu.doctor

---

## Client

**Dr. George Ungureanu**
Neurosurgeon, Romania

---

## Project Summary

A professional medical website for a neurosurgeon, built to serve patients and their families as their primary audience. The website communicates clinical expertise through the lens of patient-centered care — making complex medical information accessible, calming, and actionable.

---

## Project Purpose

Patients facing neurosurgical conditions experience high levels of fear, confusion, and uncertainty. They search the internet for information they cannot find in a medical office visit. They need a doctor they can trust before they ever meet one.

This website bridges that gap. It positions Dr. Ungureanu as a trusted guide — not a distant authority — and gives patients the information, clarity, and confidence they need to take the next step.

---

## What This Website Is

- A patient resource and trust-building platform
- A clear, empathetic guide through neurosurgical conditions and processes
- A credible, academic introduction to the doctor as a person and clinician
- A low-friction pathway from "searching for answers" to "booking a consultation"

---

## What This Website Is Not

- A CV or academic portfolio
- A marketing funnel
- A self-promotional monument
- A medical conference page
- A corporate healthcare product

## Content Priority Order

1. Clarity — patients understand what they're reading
2. Empathy — patients feel seen and not alone
3. Guidance — patients know what to do next
4. Trust — patients feel confident in the doctor
5. Academic credibility — peers and referrers recognize expertise
6. Professional achievements — visible but not dominant

---

## Design References

The design should feel like it belongs alongside:
- Mayo Clinic (mayoclinic.org)
- Johns Hopkins Medicine (hopkinsmedicine.org)
- Cleveland Clinic (my.clevelandclinic.org)
- Stanford Medicine (stanfordmedicine.org)
- The New England Journal of Medicine (nejm.org)

These references share: editorial restraint, generous whitespace, authoritative typography, warm but clinical palette, zero marketing noise.

---

## Success Criteria

**Primary:** A patient visits the website and says:
> "I felt calmer and more informed after visiting this website."

**Secondary:**
- Time on page increases on condition and treatment pages
- Appointment requests increase
- Referral doctors mention the website favorably
- Bounce rate is low on condition pages (patients read, not scan)

**Failure signal:**
> "This looks impressive, but I don't know what to do next."

---

## Constraints

- No startup aesthetics
- No aggressive marketing language
- No flashy animations or transitions
- No stock-photo medical clichés
- No unexplained medical abbreviations
- No language that intimidates instead of reassures
- No design that centers the doctor over the patient

---

## Scope (Phase 1)

Phase 1 covers the documentation foundation and design system only. No implementation files, no WordPress templates, no Elementor JSON.

Phase 2 will cover the global design system implementation in Elementor.

Phase 3 will cover page templates and content entry.
