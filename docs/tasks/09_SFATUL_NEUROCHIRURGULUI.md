# 1. Purpose

## 1.1 Why This Section Exists

A patient who receives a neurological diagnosis experiences a specific and acute form of information poverty: they suddenly need to understand something complex and frightening about their own body, and almost nothing in their environment is designed to help them do that well. Internet searches return technical papers written for clinicians, forum posts from patients whose situations differ in crucial ways, and advertising from private clinics. Nothing is written by the neurosurgeon who might treat them, in a voice that prioritizes understanding over impressiveness.

Sfatul Neurochirurgului exists to be that resource. It is built from the premise that a patient who understands their condition, their options, and what to expect before they sit across from a clinician — is a patient who can participate in their own care. They can ask informed questions. They can evaluate explanations. They can make decisions. Fear is not eliminated by understanding, but it is substantially reduced. A patient who has read what a disc herniation is, what imaging reveals, and what a first consultation involves — arrives differently than a patient who has not.

This is not a secondary purpose of the website. It is why the Sfatul Neurochirurgului section occupies the third position in the navigation, appears as a preview section on the homepage, and is represented in the professional timeline on /despre as a named career milestone. It is the most enduring and scalable proof that Dr. Ungureanu's patient-centered commitment is genuine and sustained.

## 1.2 Relationship with YouTube, Instagram, and Facebook

Sfatul Neurochirurgului is not born on this website. It exists already — as videos on YouTube, as educational posts on Instagram and Facebook, as content that has already found an audience that seeks it out. These social channels have their own audiences, their own algorithms, their own discovery mechanisms.

The website serves a different and complementary function:

**Social channels:** Discovery and reach. A patient who does not know Dr. Ungureanu encounters an educational video through a search or a recommendation. They learn something. They may or may not follow the channel.

**The website:** Organization and depth. The same educational content — and more — is organized here by topic, by question, by situation. A patient who has a specific need (preparing for surgery, supporting a family member, looking up a term their neurologist used) can navigate to that content directly, without needing to browse a YouTube channel or scroll an Instagram feed.

The website does not replace the social channels. It makes the educational mission accessible to people who do not, or cannot, find it through social discovery — and it organizes it in a way that a scrolling social feed cannot.

**The practical relationship:**

| Channel | Role | Content type | Audience |
|---------|------|-------------|---------|
| YouTube | Primary video platform | Long-form educational videos | Active followers + YouTube search |
| Instagram | Awareness + short-form | Educational snippets, infographics, Q&A | Younger patient-adjacent audience |
| Facebook | Community reach | Shared videos, posts, discussion | Older demographic, family members |
| Website /sfatul-neurochirurgului | Organization + depth | Full articles, FAQ, glossary, curated media | Patients seeking specific information |

**Phase 2 connection:** Make.com automation creates a WordPress Draft from social media publications (YouTube, Instagram, Facebook) for administrator review and optional promotion to the SN Articles section. This is not automatic publication — it is assisted curation. The workflow is defined in Section 12.

## 1.3 Why Education Reduces Patient Anxiety

The connection between knowledge and reduced anxiety is not generic — it is specific to the neurosurgical patient experience.

**The information vacuum problem:** When a patient receives a diagnosis and leaves the clinic, they enter an information vacuum. The physician has explained what they could in the time available. The patient, still in the shock of the news, has retained a fraction of it. They go home and do not know what to ask, what to look up, or who to trust. The Sfatul Neurochirurgului content addresses this vacuum directly — it is available at 10pm when the patient cannot sleep, on the phone, in the patient's language, written to be understood without a medical degree.

**Specific anxiety reduction mechanisms:**
- **Named fear reduction:** A patient who reads "Ce este hernia de disc" and understands the anatomy and mechanics of what has happened to them is less frightened by the diagnosis than a patient holding a term they cannot interpret
- **Process clarity:** A patient who reads "Cum decurge prima consultație" arrives knowing what to expect — reducing the anxiety of the unknown that surrounds any unfamiliar medical encounter
- **Permission to be uncertain:** Educational content that acknowledges what is not known, what varies by patient, and what requires case-specific evaluation — normalizes the uncertainty that a patient feels rather than falsely eliminating it
- **Preparation for dialogue:** A patient who has read about their options arrives at the consultation prepared to ask questions — which transforms the consultation from a pronouncement from authority into a dialogue between a patient and their clinician

## 1.4 Why This Is a Brand Pillar, Not a Content Category

A content category is a label applied to posts on a website. It organizes content for browsing. It can be added, removed, renamed.

A brand pillar is a sustained, named commitment that shapes the practice's identity. Sfatul Neurochirurgului is a brand pillar because:
- It predates the website and exists independently of it
- It has a specific name, a consistent voice, and an established audience across platforms
- It appears in the professional timeline on /despre as a career milestone — signaling that Dr. Ungureanu treats it as a professional achievement, not an SEO tactic
- It is referenced in the navigation at position 3 — after Acasă and Afecțiuni, before Recomandări and Despre — giving it equal prominence to the clinical content sections
- Its existence is itself a trust signal: a doctor who has dedicated sustained effort to public education, before this website existed, is demonstrating something about his values that cannot be manufactured retroactively

The consequence of treating this as a brand pillar rather than a content category:
- Content is never published to fill volume quotas or maintain posting frequency
- Every piece of content is evaluated against the question: "Does this help a patient understand something important?" — not "Does this attract search traffic?"
- The section's identity is consistent across all platforms — the voice, the register, and the purpose are the same on YouTube as they are on the website
- The section is presented with the same care and design quality as every other primary section of the site — not as an afterthought appended to the clinical content

## 1.5 Emotional Goals

**Primary:** A patient who navigates to /sfatul-neurochirurgului should feel that they have found a resource made specifically for them — that this is a place where their questions are anticipated and answered by the doctor who might treat them, not by a generic medical information platform.

**Secondary emotional goals:**
- Relief that authoritative, trustworthy information exists and is accessible without friction
- Recognition that their fear and uncertainty are normal and anticipated — the content is organized around questions patients actually have, not around anatomical categories that make sense only to clinicians
- Calm — the educational content is organized and readable, not alarming or dense
- Confidence that they can prepare for their consultation in a meaningful way

## 1.6 Educational Goals

- Patients leave with a better understanding of their condition than they arrived with
- Patients understand what the consultation process involves before they arrive
- Family members understand how to support the patient in practical and emotional ways
- Patients and families understand the vocabulary that will be used in clinical conversations
- Patients understand what recovery involves before they commit to a procedure

## 1.7 Trust Goals

- Sustained and organized educational content demonstrates that Dr. Ungureanu's patient-centered commitment is genuine — not asserted in a philosophy statement but evidenced in a library of educational work
- The quality and specificity of the content demonstrates clinical depth — a doctor who can explain complex neurosurgical concepts in plain language has a secure command of those concepts
- The family-oriented content demonstrates that the practice understands patients as people with support networks, not as isolated cases

## 3.1 What This Hero Communicates

The /sfatul-neurochirurgului hero is different from the /recomandari hero and the /despre hero. It is the entrance to an educational brand — one that already exists and already has an audience. The hero acknowledges this: it does not introduce Sfatul Neurochirurgului as a new thing. It welcomes the visitor to an organized home for something they may already know.

The hero serves three simultaneous audiences:
- **Patients who have never heard of Sfatul Neurochirurgului** — they arrive from Google, from /recomandari, or from the homepage preview. For them, the hero establishes: this is a place of organized educational content about neurosurgery, written by the doctor.
- **Patients who follow Sfatul Neurochirurgului on social media** — they arrive knowing the brand. The hero confirms: this is the same voice, organized differently.
- **Family members** — they arrive looking for help understanding someone else's situation. The hero must signal that they are also welcome and that content exists specifically for them.

# 5. Featured Educational Content

**Component:** `organism-article-grid` (featured variant)
**Background:** `color-surface-warm` (`#F4EFE6`)
**Position:** Section 3
**Visibility:** Hidden until ≥3 SN Articles are published (consistent with homepage Section 4 visibility rule). When hidden, no placeholder is shown.

## 5.1 Purpose

The featured section is the editorial lead of the hub page. It answers: *Of everything here, what should I read first?* Not "what is newest" — what is most useful to the patient who arrives at this page today.

This is a curated editorial decision made by the administrator, in consultation with Dr. Ungureanu. It is not an algorithm. It is not automatic. No article becomes featured because it was recently published or because it received many views.

## 5.2 Number of Featured Items

**3 featured articles on initial launch.** Consistent with the homepage preview (which shows 3 SN Articles). This number creates a complete, balanced visual grid at desktop viewport widths.

**Maximum 4 featured articles** after the SN Article library grows beyond 10 articles. Beyond 4, the "featured" designation loses its editorial meaning — if half the articles are featured, nothing is featured.

**Minimum 3 featured articles** for the section to be visible. If fewer than 3 articles are published, the section is hidden (same rule as homepage Section 4). A section of 1 or 2 "featured" articles creates an unbalanced layout and implies a thinner content library than the page should present.

## 5.3 Selection Principles

**What makes an article featured:**

1. **Universal relevance:** The article addresses something a large proportion of patients visiting this site need to understand — not a narrow subspecialty topic appropriate only for specific cases
2. **Anxiety reduction:** Articles that reduce the most common and most acute patient fears are prioritized: what is a first consultation, what does an MRI show, what is recovery like
3. **Entry-level accessibility:** Featured articles are those that require the least prior knowledge. Depth and specificity are valuable in the library — the featured section is the beginning, not the advanced level
4. **Freshness consideration:** When two articles are otherwise equally appropriate for featuring, a more recently updated article is preferred — but newness alone is never the selection criterion

**What does NOT make an article featured:**
- Recency of publication
- Quantity of social media shares
- Connection to current trending medical topics
- SEO-driven keyword density

## 5.4 Featured Article Card Content

Each featured article card contains:
- **Category indicator:** `atom-label` in `color-accent` background — "RECUPERARE", "INVESTIGAȚII", etc. — the SN Article CPT category
- **Article title:** `type-h3` (Lora 26px / 700) — the article's full title, not truncated
- **Excerpt:** `type-body` (Inter 17px), 2–3 sentences. The article's excerpt field from the SN Article CPT.
- **Read time:** `type-body-sm` (Inter 15px), optional — "~5 minute" — displays if the field is populated in the CPT
- **Link:** `atom-link-arrow` → individual article page at `/sfatul-neurochirurgului/[slug]`

No author attribution on featured cards — the author is always Dr. Ungureanu; it is understood, not repeated on every card.

No publication date on featured cards. Dates signal "this may be outdated." Educational content is reviewed and updated — it does not age the way news content does. Dates are available in the SN Article CPT for admin reference but are not displayed on article cards.

## 5.5 Rotation Strategy

Featured articles are updated by the administrator when:
- New SN Articles are published that are sufficiently foundational to earn featured placement
- The content library grows to a point where different articles are more relevant to the current patient population
- Dr. Ungureanu identifies that a particular topic is generating many questions in consultations — suggesting that article should be foregrounded

**No automatic rotation.** There is no scheduled refresh, no A/B rotation, no algorithm. The administrator makes a deliberate editorial decision to change featured articles. This decision is logged in the WordPress admin (by changing the `featured` field in the SN Article CPT from true to false on the departing article and true on the incoming article).

## 5.6 Section Header

Above the featured grid:
- `atom-overline`: "LECTURI RECOMANDATE" (or equivalent — final wording from Dr. Ungureanu)
- `type-h2`: "Resurse educative esențiale" (or equivalent)
- Optional 1-sentence editorial note: e.g., "O selecție a articolelor care răspund celor mai frecvente întrebări ale pacienților noștri." — Not marketing copy.

# 6. Videoclipuri

**Component:** `organism-video-grid`
**Background:** `color-surface` (`#FDFBF7`)
**Position:** Section 4
**Visibility:** Hidden until ≥1 curated video entry exists in WordPress

## 6.1 What Videos Are on This Page

The Videoclipuri section is not a YouTube channel embed. It is a curated collection of educational videos from the Sfatul Neurochirurgului YouTube channel, organized by topic, presented within the site's design system.

The distinction matters:
- A YouTube channel embed presents the channel in YouTube's own interface — with YouTube's branding, YouTube's recommendations, YouTube's ads, and YouTube's algorithmic sidebar. The patient leaves the site.
- A curated video collection presents specific, selected videos in the site's visual language — without YouTube's commercial and algorithmic overlay. The patient stays on the page, navigates the content the doctor has selected.

## 6.2 Phase 1 — Manual Curation

In Phase 1, video entries are created manually in WordPress:
- Administrator creates a Media Item CPT entry (or a Video-specific CPT entry, depending on Phase 3 implementation decision) with:
  - Video title
  - YouTube URL (the source URL — not an embed code)
  - Brief description (2–3 sentences in patient language)
  - Category (simptome / consultații / investigații / intervenții / recuperare / familie)
  - Thumbnail image (either a custom upload or the YouTube auto-thumbnail, stored locally)
  - Display order (manual ordering by admin)

The page renders the thumbnail + title + description. On click/tap, the video opens:
- **Option A:** In a modal/lightbox on the page, as a YouTube embed that loads on demand (lazy-loaded; no preload; not playing until the patient presses the play button)
- **Option B:** In a new tab at the YouTube URL

Phase 3 implementation determines which option serves the user experience better. **Either way: no autoplay. The video never plays until the patient explicitly presses play.** A video that begins playing audio on a page visited by a frightened patient in a quiet room is an experience failure. See WCAG 1.4.2 (Audio Control).

## 6.3 Phase 2 — Automation via Make

Phase 2 introduces a Make.com scenario that monitors the YouTube channel for new publications and creates a WordPress Draft in the SN Articles or Media Hub CPT for administrator review.

The workflow is:
```
New video published on YouTube channel
       ↓
Make.com detects publication (YouTube trigger — new video in channel)
       ↓
Make.com creates WordPress Draft entry:
  · Title: [Video title from YouTube]
  · Content: [Video description from YouTube]
  · YouTube URL: [YouTube video URL]
  · Thumbnail: [YouTube thumbnail URL — stored locally]
  · Status: draft (never auto-publish)
  · Category: [requires admin assignment — cannot be automated reliably]
       ↓
Admin email notification: "Nouveau video YouTube — révision en attente"
       ↓
Administrator reviews draft in WordPress admin:
  · Assigns category
  · Reviews/edits description for website register (may differ from YouTube description)
  · Adds display_order
  · Sets status → publish (or discards draft if not appropriate for website)
```

This workflow is automated delivery of content to the review queue — not automated publication. No video appears on the website without an administrator's explicit publish decision.

## 6.4 Relationship with Media Hub and Educational Articles

**Videoclipuri vs. Media Hub:**
- Videoclipuri: long-form educational videos from YouTube. Full videos (5–30 minutes). Topic-organized.
- Media Hub: short-form social content — Instagram posts, Facebook shares, YouTube Shorts or clips. Snippets and snapshots, not full educational videos.

**Videoclipuri vs. SN Articles:**
- Some videos are accompanied by, or become the basis for, a full written SN Article. The video is the entry point; the article provides depth that video cannot.
- Some content works better in video form only — demonstrations, walkthroughs, Q&A formats. These live in Videoclipuri without requiring a written companion.
- Some SN Articles are based on written content without a video counterpart — written answers to questions that do not require visual explanation.

The SN Article CPT has a `video_url` optional field — articles with an associated video display the video within the article page, not only in the Videoclipuri section.

## 6.5 No Embedded Autoplay Videos — Anywhere on the Site

This is a site-wide rule documented here for completeness:
- No video, anywhere on georgeungureanu.doctor, begins playing without the patient's explicit action
- No video has `autoplay` attribute set to true, whether muted or not
- YouTube embeds use the `?autoplay=0` parameter and `?mute=1` is never used as a workaround for autoplay rules
- Autoplaying muted video is still a WCAG 1.4.2 violation in spirit — it introduces unexpected visual motion to a page a patient is trying to read

# 7. Întrebări Frecvente

**Component:** `organism-faq-section` (6 categories)
**Background:** `color-surface-warm` (`#F4EFE6`)
**Position:** Section 5
**Visibility:** Visible when FAQ content is provided by Dr. Ungureanu

## 7.1 Purpose

The FAQ section is one of the highest-value sections on this page. It is organized not by medical taxonomy but by the patient's journey — the sequence of questions a patient has as they move from symptom awareness to consultation to possible procedure to recovery.

A patient in the symptom phase asks different questions than a patient preparing for surgery. Organizing the FAQ by patient phase — not by anatomical structure or procedure type — means a patient can navigate to their current situation and find the questions that match where they are.

## 7.2 Six Categories

| Category | Romanian label | When a patient uses this | Example questions |
|----------|---------------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| Simptome | "Simptome" | Early: something is wrong and I don't know what | "When do I need to see a neurologist vs. a neurosurgeon?" / "Is my headache serious enough to warrant investigation?" / "What symptoms should prompt me to go to emergency?" |
| Consultații | "Consultații" | Before or after a first consultation | "What will happen at my first consultation?" / "How long does a consultation last?" / "Should I bring someone with me?" |
| Investigații | "Investigații" | When imaging has been ordered | "What is the difference between RMN and CT?" / "Why do I need contrast?" / "Is the radiation dangerous?" |
| Intervenții | "Intervenții chirurgicale" | When surgery is being discussed | "What does 'minimally invasive' mean?" / "How do I know if surgery is necessary?" / "What is the recovery period after disc surgery?" |
| Recuperare | "Recuperare" | During or after treatment | "When can I return to normal activity?" / "What should I watch for after a procedure?" / "Is it normal to feel worse before better?" |
| Familie | "Familie și aparținători" | Family members accompanying or supporting the patient | "How do I help a family member without overwhelming them?" / "Can I attend the consultation?" / "What should I ask the doctor when I accompany my parent?" |

## 7.3 Structure of the FAQ Component

**Navigation:** The 6 categories are presented as horizontal tab-style navigation above the FAQ entries. On mobile, this becomes a vertical stacked list or a scrollable horizontal chip row. The patient selects a category and the relevant questions appear below.

**Within each category:** Individual Q&A pairs presented as single-level accordion items. One click opens the answer; the same click closes it. Only one answer can be open at a time within a category (simplicity for cognitive accessibility).

**No nested accordions.** The category is the first level. The question is the second level. The answer opens within the question item. No further nesting exists. If a question requires a detailed answer that contains sub-questions, it is split into multiple distinct Q&A pairs.

**Answer format:** Plain Romanian. `type-body` (Inter 17px). Maximum 150 words per answer. If an answer requires more than 150 words, it likely belongs in a full SN Article rather than an FAQ — link to the article from within the answer.

# 9. Recuperare și Pregătire

**Component:** `organism-educational-block` (recovery variant)
**Background:** `color-surface-warm` (`#F4EFE6`)
**Position:** Section 7
**Visibility:** Always visible — requires content

## 9.1 What This Section Covers

Recovery and preparation for procedures are among the highest-anxiety topics in neurosurgery. A patient who is considering a procedure needs to understand what recovery involves before they can make an informed decision. A patient who has already had a procedure needs to understand what is normal, what is not, and who to contact if they are concerned.

This section serves both audiences — distinguishing content by phase (before vs. after) clearly.

## 9.2 Content Model

| Topic | Content direction | Critical constraints |
|-------|------------------|---------------------|
| Înainte de intervenție | What the patient should do and avoid in the weeks before a procedure — physical preparation, medication management, arrangements at home, preparation for the recovery period | Specific actions, not medical advice; coordinate with patient's care team |
| Imediat după intervenție | The first hours and days — what to expect physically, who will be around, what the patient can and cannot do | Written to normalize; not alarming; acknowledges discomfort without catastrophizing |
| Recuperare la domiciliu | The weeks following a procedure — activities, limitations, milestones, medical appointments, nutrition, rest | Written with genuine variability acknowledged: recovery differs by procedure, by patient |
| Recuperare cu specialistul | When and why physical rehabilitation is part of recovery; what a physiotherapist's role is in neurological recovery; how to access rehabilitation services | Collaborative, not prescriptive |
| Semne de alarmă | Specific symptoms that require immediate contact with the medical team or emergency services | This content must be specific and clear; vagueness in this section is dangerous |
| Așteptări realiste | The emotional and physical reality that recovery takes longer than expected, that there are setbacks, that "feeling worse before better" is common | Manages expectations without discouraging; does not promise timelines |

## 9.3 No Medical Guarantees

This section must not imply predictable outcomes or definitive recovery timelines. Every procedure has a different recovery profile. Every patient has a different baseline. Co-existing conditions affect recovery.

**Language to avoid:**
- "Veți fi complet recuperat în 6 săptămâni" — this is a guarantee
- "Marea majoritate a pacienților se recuperează fără complicații" — this is statistical reassurance that the patient cannot apply to their own case
- "Urmând aceste instrucțiuni, recuperarea va fi..." — causal implication the content cannot support

**Language that works:**
- "Recuperarea variază semnificativ de la pacient la pacient și de la tip de intervenție la alta."
- "Dr. Ungureanu va discuta cu dvs. ce să așteptați specific pentru situația dvs. înainte de externare."
- "Semnele de mai jos pot apărea sau nu — contactați echipa medicală dacă observați..."

## 9.4 Warning Signs Section

The "Semne de alarmă" content is the most clinically sensitive content on this page. It must be:
- **Specific:** Name the signs. "Durere crescândă," "febră," "pierderea bruscă a sensibilității" — not "dacă vă simțiți mai rău."
- **Actionable:** For each warning sign, the appropriate action: "contactați imediat echipa medicală la numărul primit la externare" or "prezentați-vă de urgență la urgența neurochirurgicală."
- **Clear about severity:** Distinguish between "contact your care team during working hours" and "go to emergency immediately."
- **Reviewed by Dr. Ungureanu:** This content is specifically reviewed and approved by the doctor before publication. It is not drafted by an administrator from general medical sources.

# 10. Pentru Familie și Aparținători

**Component:** `organism-educational-block` (family variant)
**Background:** `color-surface` (`#FDFBF7`)
**Position:** Section 8
**Visibility:** Always visible — requires content

## 10.1 Why Family Members Are a Primary Audience

Every analysis of who uses this website confirms that patients do not arrive alone — not logistically, and not informationally. A patient who has received a serious neurological diagnosis frequently involves a spouse, an adult child, or a close friend in their care decisions. These family members:
- Research the doctor on behalf of the patient
- Book the appointment on behalf of the patient
- Accompany the patient to consultations
- Ask questions the patient forgets or is too frightened to ask
- Manage the practical aspects of recovery at home
- Carry their own anxiety alongside the patient's

Family members are often in a state of informed helplessness: they know their relative is suffering and they do not know what to do. The medical system is not designed to address this. Physicians speak primarily to the patient. Literature is addressed to the patient. Family members are expected to absorb information from the consultation without specialized guidance.

This section exists because a well-supported patient recovers better. A family member who knows how to help effectively is not hovering and creating anxiety — they are contributing to the patient's wellbeing in ways that actually work.

## 10.2 Content Model

| Topic | Content direction | Why this matters |
|-------|------------------|-----------------|
| Cum îl/o ajut fără să copleșesc | Specific guidance: what kinds of help are wanted and what kinds create pressure; how to ask rather than assume; the difference between being present and managing the situation | Many family members inadvertently increase patient anxiety through over-management |
| Ce întrebări să adresez medicului | A list of questions family members should consider asking — different from what the patient asks — focused on practical care, recovery logistics, warning signs, and the family member's own limits | Family members often leave consultations without the information they needed because they didn't know it was appropriate to ask |
| Sprijin emoțional | How to talk about the situation without minimizing or catastrophizing; how to be present without projecting; how to acknowledge fear without amplifying it; when to seek support for themselves | Family members' unaddressed anxiety becomes a burden for the patient |
| Sprijin practic | Recovery logistics at home: home modifications, transport, medication management, appointment coordination, work leave considerations | Practical support that is actually useful rather than well-intentioned but disruptive |
| Semne de alarmă la domiciliu | What family members should watch for during home recovery — specific behavioral, physical, and neurological changes that require contacting the care team | Family members are the first observers of post-procedure changes; their ability to recognize warning signs is clinically significant |
| Recuperare la domiciliu — rolul familiei | The family's role in creating an environment that supports recovery: routine, rest, activity levels, diet, social contact — what helps and what hinders | Written with genuine variability; not a protocol; a set of principles |
| Grijă pentru aparținători | An explicit acknowledgment that caring for a seriously ill family member is psychologically demanding; guidance on maintaining the caregiver's own health; resources for family caregiver support | This content signals to the family member: we see you, not only your relative |

## 10.3 Tone for Family Members

The family audience is not less educated than the patient audience — they are differently situated. They may have more emotional distance from the diagnosis, which means they can absorb more information — but they also carry secondary trauma and guilt that the patient does not.

The tone for family-oriented content:
- Addresses the family member directly ("Dacă sunteți apropiatul unui pacient care...") not in third person
- Acknowledges that this is emotionally demanding — not only for the patient
- Does not present family support as a simple set of instructions to be followed — acknowledges that every family dynamic is different
- Does not position the family member as the patient's manager or caregiver-by-default — acknowledges that the patient retains agency and the family member's role is support, not control

# 11. Glosar Medical

**Component:** `organism-glossary`
**Background:** `color-surface-warm` (`#F4EFE6`)
**Position:** Section 9
**Visibility:** Visible when ≥10 glossary entries are provided by Dr. Ungureanu

## 11.1 Purpose

Medical language is one of the most significant barriers between a patient and their ability to participate in their own care. A patient who hears "stenoză lombară" or "radiculopatie" and does not understand the terms cannot ask informed questions, cannot research their own situation, and cannot evaluate the information they are given.

The glossary does not replace medical vocabulary — it translates it. A patient who has read the glossary definition of "stenoză" arrives at their consultation knowing what the doctor means when they use the term. They can follow the explanation. They can ask where the stenosis is, how severe it is, and what the options are.

## 11.2 Entry Format

Each glossary entry contains:

```
[Term] — type-h4 (Inter 20px / 600) — the medical term, in Romanian or Latin as used clinically
[Plain Romanian definition] — type-body (Inter 17px) — 1–3 sentences, no medical vocabulary without self-explanation
[Example] — type-body (Inter 17px), italic — "De exemplu: ..." — a concrete example that grounds the definition
[Related terms] — atom-link to related glossary entries — optional, maximum 2–3 links
[Pronunciation guide] — optional, only for terms patients may be embarrassed to mispronounce
```

## 11.3 Glossary Rules

**Plain Romanian, always:** Every definition is in the clearest possible Romanian. Definitions do not use other unexplained medical terms. If defining "mielita" requires mentioning "măduva spinării," that term is assumed understood — or it has its own entry.

**Short, not exhaustive:** A glossary entry is not a medical encyclopedia article. 1–3 sentences of definition + 1 example is the target. If more explanation is needed, write an SN Article and link to it.

**Examples are mandatory:** Abstract definitions are difficult to retain. A concrete example — "De exemplu: o hernie de disc la nivelul L4-L5 poate provoca durere în picior, pe traseul nervului sciatic" — fixes the definition in the patient's mind.

**No editorial voice in definitions:** Glossary entries are factual, not persuasive. They do not reassure ("this is usually treatable") and do not alarm ("this can be serious"). They define.

**Reviewed by Dr. Ungureanu:** All glossary entries are reviewed and approved by the doctor before publication. A clinically inaccurate definition is worse than no definition.

## 11.4 Organization

**Phase 1:** Alphabetical list. A-Z tab navigation allows the patient to jump to the relevant letter. On mobile, the tabs are replaced by a search field or a scrollable letter chip row.

**Phase 2:** Category-based organization in addition to alphabetical — terms grouped by condition category, by body part, or by procedure type, allowing a patient to study the vocabulary for their specific situation.

**Target volume Phase 1:** 30–60 entries. Sufficient to cover the most common terms encountered in neurosurgical consultations. Not a complete medical dictionary.

**Target volume Phase 2:** 100–200 entries. Expanded to cover the full vocabulary of conditions represented in the Afecțiuni CPT and the SN Article content library.

## 11.5 Integration with the Rest of the Site

In Phase 2, glossary terms that appear within SN Articles and condition pages become inline links — clicking or tapping a term reveals the glossary definition in a tooltip or a small drawer, without navigating away from the current page. This requires Phase 2 planning and implementation.

In Phase 1, the glossary is a standalone section. SN Articles and condition content may link to the glossary section with "Consultați glosarul medical" — a simple hyperlink, not an inline tooltip.

# 13. Editorial Principles

## 13.1 Clarity Before Completeness

A patient does not need an exhaustive account of a medical topic. They need the specific information relevant to their specific situation, in a form they can understand and act on.

This means:
- An SN Article about disc herniation does not need to cover every type of disc herniation, every possible treatment, and every variant of anatomy. It covers what a patient who has been told they have a disc herniation needs to understand right now.
- A FAQ answer does not need to address every exception and edge case. It addresses the question as asked, with a note that individual situations vary and the consultation is the appropriate place for specificity.
- The glossary entry for a term does not reproduce the Wikipedia article on that term. It answers: "what does this word mean in the context of a neurosurgical consultation?"

**The test:** Can a patient who reads this piece of content say: "I understand this enough to have a better conversation with my doctor"? If yes, the content is complete enough. If it contains more information than is necessary for that outcome, it is unnecessarily complex.

## 13.2 Empathy Before Authority

Educational content written from a position of clinical authority — "the correct approach to disc herniation is..." — positions the patient as a recipient of information, not a participant in their care. It creates distance.

Educational content written from a position of empathy — "many patients wonder whether their back pain requires imaging, and the answer depends on specific factors that a consultation can evaluate" — positions the patient as the subject of the content, not its object. It creates connection.

This does not mean the content is soft or vague. It means the starting point is the patient's question, the patient's fear, the patient's situation — not the correct clinical taxonomy.

**In practice:**
- Begin with the patient's question ("De ce mă doare mâna dacă problema este în coloana cervicală?"), not with the anatomical explanation
- Acknowledge uncertainty when it is genuine — "for most patients, but not all, the first consultation is sufficient to determine whether imaging is necessary"
- Use "dvs." and "dumneavoastră" — address the patient directly throughout

## 13.3 Explanation Before Terminology

Every medical term introduced in educational content must be explained before it is used, or immediately after it is introduced. There are no "it is assumed the reader knows what X means" moments in this content library.

The pattern: term introduced → definition in parentheses or in the next sentence → used subsequently in text.

Example of the anti-pattern: "In stenoze de canal lombar, simptomele de claudicație neurogenă apar bilateral."
Example of the correct pattern: "Stenoza de canal lombar — îngustarea canalului prin care trece măduva spinării în zona lombară — poate provoca o senzație de slăbiciune sau durere la mers care forțează pacientul să se oprească și să se aplece înainte. Aceasta se numește claudicație neurogenă."

## 13.4 Guidance Before Persuasion

Content on this site does not try to persuade patients to choose a particular treatment, to seek care urgently, or to prefer any approach over another. It guides — it helps patients understand their options, understand what questions to ask, and understand what to expect.

The difference:
- **Persuasion:** "Hernia de disc operată la timp previne complicațiile permanente." (Implies urgency, creates fear-of-missing-out)
- **Guidance:** "Decizia de a opera o hernie de disc depinde de mai mulți factori — tipul simptomelor, durata, răspunsul la tratamentul conservator — pe care Dr. Ungureanu îi evaluează cu fiecare pacient."

Guidance respects the patient's autonomy. Persuasion removes it.

## 13.5 Patient First — Not SEO First

Content is written to answer the patient's question. Not to rank for a specific keyword. Not to include a term a specific number of times. Not to be a certain length because SEO tools recommend it.

This does not mean SEO is ignored — well-organized, clearly written educational content will naturally attract search traffic because it answers questions patients are actually asking. But the editorial decision is always: "What does the patient need to understand?" — never "What does the algorithm need to see?"

**Practical consequences:**
- Article length is determined by what the topic requires — not by a word count target
- Topic selection is determined by what patients actually ask in consultations — not by keyword research tools
- Headlines are written in patient language — not in keyword-dense phrasing that no patient would search for
- Content is organized for reading — not for scanning-to-extract-keywords

## 13.6 Reading Level

All Sfatul Neurochirurgului content should be understandable by a person with a high school education. This is not a ceiling — it is a floor. A patient with a medical background will find the content accessible and not condescending. A patient with no medical background will be able to read and understand it.

**Tools for assessing reading level:**
- Sentence length: maximum 15–18 words on average. Some sentences may be shorter for emphasis; none should routinely exceed 25 words.
- Paragraph length: maximum 4–5 lines on desktop. Mobile paragraph length is shorter — 3–4 lines maximum.
- Passive voice: avoided. "Dr. Ungureanu evaluates each patient's imaging" rather than "Each patient's imaging is evaluated."
- Abstract nouns: replaced with concrete language wherever possible.

```
H1: Sfatul Neurochirurgului (hero)
  H2: [Introductory message heading]
  H2: Resurse educative esențiale (Featured content)
    H3: [Article card titles — 3–4 entries]
  H2: Videoclipuri
    H3: [Video titles — if videos are presented with headings; otherwise strong text]
  H2: Întrebări frecvente
    H3: [FAQ category names — Simptome, Consultații, etc.]
      [Q&A accordion — question text in button element, not a heading]
  H2: Prima consultație
    H3: [Sub-topic headings within section]
  H2: Recuperare și pregătire
    H3: [Sub-topic headings]
  H2: Pentru familie și aparținători
    H3: [Sub-topic headings]
  H2: Glosar medical
    H4: [Glossary terms — or strong text if heading count is excessive]
  H2: Din activitatea Sfatul Neurochirurgului (Media Hub)
  H2: [Final CTA heading]
```

**Glossary terms as H4:** With 30–60 glossary entries, making each term an H4 creates 30–60 headings within the glossary section — which may create an overwhelming screen reader navigation experience. Alternative: `role="term"` + `role="definition"` within a description list (`<dl>`) structure, with the term as `<dt>` (bold) and the definition as `<dd>`. This provides semantic structure without overloading the heading outline. Phase 3 implementation decision.

## 15.2 FAQ Accordion Accessibility

Documented in detail in Section 7.5 above. Summary:
- Category navigation keyboard accessible
- Each accordion item is a `<button>` with `aria-expanded` and `aria-controls`
- Answer panels have matching `id` and `aria-hidden`
- Reduced motion: instant show/hide without animation
- JavaScript-off fallback: all answers visible

## 15.3 Screen Reader Support

| Element | Screen reader treatment |
|---------|------------------------|
| Hero (typographic — no photography) | Reads naturally; overline text is not a heading — `aria-hidden` if decorative, included if meaningful |
| Featured article cards | Each card wrapped in `<article>`. Category label, title, excerpt, and read-time read in order. Link at end: "Citiți: [article title]" |
| Video cards | `role="article"` or `<article>`. Thumbnail has alt text: "[Video title] — videoclip YouTube". Action link: "Urmăriți pe YouTube: [title], se deschide într-o fereastră nouă" |
| FAQ category navigation | `role="tablist"` with individual `role="tab"` elements. Active tab has `aria-selected="true"`. |
| FAQ answer panels | `role="tabpanel"` with `aria-labelledby` pointing to the category tab |
| Glossary (as `<dl>`) | Screen readers navigate by `<dt>` terms; definitions follow naturally |
| Media Hub cards | Thumbnail alt text: "[platform] post — [caption first 60 characters]". Link: "Vizualizați pe [platform], se deschide într-o fereastră nouă" |
| External links | All external links (YouTube, Instagram, Facebook, source URLs) include screen-reader-only text: "se deschide într-o fereastră nouă" or `title` attribute noting external destination |

## 15.4 Cognitive Accessibility

The hub page's length and content density create cognitive accessibility challenges. Mitigations:

- **Section landmarks:** Every section has a visible H2 that tells the patient what they are about to read
- **No hidden prerequisite:** Each section is self-contained — a patient who navigates directly to "Pentru familie" does not need to have read the preceding sections
- **Consistent card format:** All article cards follow the same format — category → title → excerpt → read time → link. Patients learn the pattern once.
- **FAQ single-answer-open:** Only one accordion answer is open at a time within a category — reduces cognitive load of tracking multiple open panels
- **Glossary alphabetical:** A predictable, familiar organization system — no learning curve
- **No time-based elements:** Nothing on the page auto-advances, auto-closes, or requires the patient to act within a time window

## 15.5 Reading Level and Typography

Reading level targets documented in Section 13.6 apply to all content on this page.

Typography minimums:
- Body text: 17px desktop / 16px mobile — never smaller
- Line height: 1.70 for body, 1.75 for introductory message
- Column max-width: 720px (68 characters)
- Inter italic is never used in body text — this is a site-wide rule (Inter has no true italic variant; faux italic is prohibited)
- Lora italic is used only for `type-quote` contexts

## 15.6 Reduced Motion

All animations on this page are suppressed under `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)`:
- FAQ accordion open/close transitions: instant
- Card hover effects: reduced to color change only (no transform)
- Media Hub card hover effects: reduced
- Any scroll-triggered entrance animations: disabled

# 16. Phase 2 Opportunities

These are documented for planning purposes. None are implemented in Phase 1.

## 16.1 Automated Content Ingestion (Make.com)

Phase 2 introduces the Make.com automation scenarios described in Sections 6.3 and 12.4: YouTube, Instagram, and Facebook posts are detected by Make, which creates WordPress Draft entries for administrator review. This reduces the manual effort of curating the Media Hub and the video library, while maintaining the non-automatic-publish principle.

**Condition:** Phase 1 CPT structure for Media Item and SN Article must be compatible with the automated Draft format. The `source_url`, `thumbnail`, `status: draft`, and `platform` fields must be present in the Phase 1 CPT for Phase 2 automation to write to them.

## 16.2 Richer Media Experiences

Phase 2 may introduce:
- **Video transcripts:** Full transcripts of educational videos, published alongside the video — increasing accessibility (deaf or hard-of-hearing patients) and SEO value
- **Audio versions of SN Articles:** Text-to-speech or recorded audio alternatives for patients who prefer listening to reading
- **Interactive anatomy diagrams:** Labeled diagrams of spinal anatomy, brain regions, or specific conditions — allowing patients to explore the anatomical context of their diagnosis

**Condition:** All interactive elements must meet WCAG 2.1 AA. Diagrams must have text alternatives. Audio alternatives must have text alternatives. No interactive element requires mouse precision — all must be operable by keyboard and touch.

## 16.3 Search Within Educational Content

Phase 2 may introduce a search field that queries the SN Article library, the glossary, and the FAQ database. A patient who searches "durere picior" finds relevant SN Articles, FAQ answers, and glossary terms in one results interface.

**Condition:** Requires sufficient content volume to make search valuable (~30+ SN Articles). Client-side search (Fuse.js or similar) is viable at this scale. Server-side search (WordPress REST API or ElasticSearch) is considered if the library exceeds 100 articles.

## 16.4 Filtering by Category and Condition

Phase 2 may introduce category and condition filters on the SN Articles section — allowing patients to browse articles relevant to their specific condition or care phase.

**Condition:** Requires the `associated_conditions` field (Condition CPT relation) to be populated on SN Articles. Filter UI must not become a dropdown (anti-pattern documented in `docs/tasks/01_INFORMATION_ARCHITECTURE.md`). Text-based category navigation is preferred.

## 16.5 Multilingual Educational Content

Phase 2 may add Hungarian-language versions of the most commonly accessed educational articles — serving the Hungarian-speaking patient population in Cluj-Napoca and the surrounding region.

**Condition:** Requires Dr. Ungureanu's decision on the target language population; a qualified translator with medical vocabulary; hreflang and language-switching implementation; and content management for a parallel language track.

## 16.6 Interactive Explainers

Phase 2 may introduce condition-specific interactive content: a patient who has been told they have a herniated disc at L4-L5 can view an interactive spinal diagram that highlights that specific vertebral level, the affected nerve root, and the typical symptom distribution.

**Condition:** Accessibility-first design — all interactive content has text equivalents. Medical accuracy reviewed by Dr. Ungureanu before publication. No gamification or quiz mechanics — this is an educational tool, not an entertainment experience.
