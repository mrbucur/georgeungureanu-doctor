## Brand Identity Summary

Dr. George Ungureanu's brand is the intersection of:

- **Clinical precision** — accuracy, expertise, and scientific rigor
- **Human warmth** — empathy, approachability, and genuine care for the patient
- **Academic gravity** — a quiet authority earned through years of specialized work
- **Romanian identity** — a distinguished physician serving Romanian patients

The resolved synthesis of these qualities is **Warm Academic Medicine**: a brand that leads with warmth and delivers authority — not the reverse.

The brand does not shout. It does not perform. It is simply, confidently present — like a good doctor in a consultation room who explains things until the patient understands.

---

## Brand Character

If the brand were a person, they would be:

- Calm under pressure
- Precise with language, never careless with words
- Genuinely interested in the person in front of them
- Confident but never arrogant
- Modern in approach, classical in values
- The doctor who explains until you understand

## Visual Identity Direction

### Overall Aesthetic

**Warm Academic Medicine** — the visual language of a distinguished medical practice that centers the patient experience. The design is modern, unhurried, editorially rigorous, and emotionally warm. It communicates: *a serious person who genuinely cares works here.*

- Warm cream backgrounds, not clinical white
- Strong typographic hierarchy with a literary serif (Lora) and a legible humanist sans (Inter)
- Deep muted sage-teal accent — calm, professional, entirely unlike pharmaceutical blue
- Restrained use of color — warmth from palette temperature, not from added hues
- Photography that centers the human encounter between doctor and patient
- Layout discipline from academic information design: clear grid, generous reading columns

### Color Philosophy

The palette is warm, calm, and considered. Not institutional blue on white. Not pharmaceutical green. Not cold hospital grey. Not luxury warmth that would feel inappropriate for a medical context.

The palette is warm cream backgrounds + warm near-black ink + deep muted sage-teal accent. Together these read as: *a thoughtful practice where the environment was chosen for the patient's comfort, not the institution's image.*

Full color system: see [`docs/design-system/COLOR_SYSTEM.md`](COLOR_SYSTEM.md)

### Typography Philosophy

Two typefaces only. Lora (serif) for editorial gravity with human warmth. Inter (sans-serif) for maximum legibility at all sizes. The contrast between them is the typographic drama of the system — it should not be supplemented.

Lora was chosen over Playfair Display because Lora's warmth precedes its authority. For a patient arriving afraid, warmth first is the correct register.

Full typography system: see [`docs/design-system/TYPOGRAPHY_SYSTEM.md`](TYPOGRAPHY_SYSTEM.md)

### Photography Direction

Photography carries the emotional weight that color supports. The photography must be genuinely human — not performed, not staged, not stock.

**Preferred:**
- Doctor listening intently during a consultation
- Doctor reviewing notes or a scan, in genuine concentration
- Doctor mid-explanation, engaged with the patient's question
- Natural or warm artificial light — no harsh studio lighting
- Real consultation room environment — bookshelves, a desk, a window
- Black and white photography considered for portrait and editorial use

**Avoid:**
- Any image that reads as staged or posed for marketing
- Operating theater or procedure imagery used decoratively
- Generic "doctor with stethoscope" or "doctor pointing at scan" compositions
- Artificially joyful patients (stock photo positivity)
- Images where equipment is more prominent than people
- Photography with cool or clinical light that conflicts with the warm palette

## Anti-Brand: What to Avoid

These patterns actively damage the brand:

| Element | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Institutional or corporate blue (any variant) | Signals generic medical website, not a personal practice |
| Clinical white backgrounds (`#FFFFFF` as primary surface) | Removes the warmth that is foundational to this direction |
| Bright primary color accents | Signals startup/corporate, not medical trust |
| Marketing-speak headlines | Erodes trust, signals self-promotion |
| Award badges and achievement icons | Shifts focus to ego, not patient care |
| Countdown timers or urgency patterns | Manipulative, inappropriate for medical context |
| Before/after photo galleries | Complex ethical territory, avoid unless carefully handled |
| Testimonial carousels with star ratings | Reduces human experience to marketing metric |
| Dense credential walls | Impresses peers, intimidates patients |
| Generic healthcare stock photos | Signals inauthenticity immediately |
| Wellness or spa aesthetics (light sage, mint, soft earth tones) | Inappropriate register for neurosurgery; undermines clinical gravity |
| Luxury or premium design signals (gold, black, editorial fashion references) | Wrong context; creates distance from the patient |
